01The degree

MS in Information Technology and Management at UT Dallas, with a focus on AI and Product Management. The program sits on exactly the intersection I'd already been living as a PM: how AI changes what products should be built, and how a PM should run that process. I finished with the 2026 Dean's Council OWLIE Award for leadership.

02AI Biz Club, 0 to 600 in eight months

UTD didn't have a builder-focused AI community when I arrived, so I started one. As Founding President I grew it from zero to 600 members in eight months, leading a 20-person organizing team. The number I actually care about is smaller: 180+ members shipped an AI app. That's activation, not sign-ups.

The club ran the first Cursor vibe-coding workshops on campus: three held, with roughly half the room coming back for the next one. That repeat rate is a retention signal, not a one-off draw. We also secured direct sponsorships and hackathon partnerships with AWS and Lovable, built so the partners drove real product adoption on campus instead of just putting a logo on a banner.

I automated the parts of running a 600-person org that don't scale by hand: onboarding, plus feedback and adoption dashboards, so growth could be measured and acted on instead of guessed at. The club was named Best Student Organization of the Year.

03Campus ambassador, Perplexity and TwinMind

In parallel with the club, I held two Campus Ambassador roles, both from May to December 2025: one for Perplexity, one for TwinMind. The job was driving real adoption on campus: running AI-productivity masterclasses, writing prompt playbooks people actually used, and embedding Perplexity Spaces directly into coursework rather than just announcing the tool existed. That distinction is the whole role. Adoption woven into how people already work sticks; an announcement doesn't.

The highlight of the TwinMind side was hosting their CEO, Daniel George, for a 100-person fireside chat: field marketing and executive relationship management, run by a student, for a room full of them.
